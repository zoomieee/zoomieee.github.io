Current focus

Designing energy-efficient analog, RF, mixed-signal, and physical-design oriented circuits with strong ties to practical IC flows. I work on analog amplifiers, OTA filters, physical design flows, and low-power mixed-signal systems.

Background

B.Tech in Electronics and Communication Engineering from IIITDM Kancheepuram, now pursuing an M.S. in Electrical and Computer Engineering at the University of Minnesota, Twin Cities.

Featured work

Low-Power Wide Swing Cascode OTA

  • Designed a differential wide swing cascode OTA in CMOS 50 nm technology.
  • Targeted low power and compact area while delivering 51.1 dB gain and 128.8 MHz UGF.
  • Validated across PVT corners with FOMs between 1175 and 1380.

Switched-Capacitor Biquad Filter for ECG

  • Implemented a high-Q (Q factor of 1.037) switched-capacitor biquad filter for low-frequency ECG amplification.
  • Designed the filter for reliable amplitude and frequency response in biomedical systems.

MCMC Processor with Knuth–Yao Sampler

  • Developed a Verilog Knuth–Yao discrete sampler core using an LFSR-based RNG.
  • Verified timing, area, and power using Synopsys Design Compiler and VCS.
  • Completed the RTL-to-GDSII flow with placement, routing, and sign-off.

2.4 GHz RF Front-End (LNA + 4-Path Mixer)

  • Designed a common-source inductive degeneration LNA achieving ~18 dB gain and 1.1 dB noise figure.
  • Implemented a 4-path passive mixer with non-overlapping LO phases for low-loss downconversion and harmonic rejection.
  • Validated performance with AC, transient, and noise simulations; evaluated gain, NF, and frequency response.
